About

Vahhab Piranfar is a molecular microbiologist and computational researcher focused on antimicrobial resistance, bacterial modeling, antibiotic PK/PD, antimicrobial pharmacometrics, and reproducible research.
Keywords

antimicrobial resistance, bacterial modeling, antibiotic PK/PD, antimicrobial pharmacometrics, Monte Carlo simulation, virtual populations, clinical microbiology, reproducible research

Professional Profile

I am a researcher specializing in medical and clinical microbiology, antimicrobial resistance, and antibiotic PK/PD modeling. Based in Jersey City, New Jersey, I combine clinical laboratory experience with bacterial-response modeling and computational evidence synthesis to study how bacterial pathogens, antibiotic exposure, and resistance interact. My work is supported by a foundation in molecular biology and bioinformatics, which I apply to translational research that connects the bench with reproducible, data-driven models.

Vision Statement

“In the modern era of microbiology, the microscope is no longer our only lens. By integrating computational data analysis with molecular techniques, we can decode pathogen behavior at a scale that was previously unimaginable.”

— Vahhab Piranfar

Research Interests

  • Antimicrobial resistance and bacterial adaptation — mechanisms of resistance and adaptation across clinical pathogens.
  • Antibiotic PK/PD and pharmacometric simulation — modeling antibiotic exposure, target attainment, and treatment response.
  • Bacterial growth, killing, persistence, and resistance modeling — quantitative description of bacterial dynamics under drug pressure.
  • Molecular diagnostics and pathogen genomics — resistance mechanisms in Brucella, Pseudomonas, and Staphylococcus.
  • Reproducible evidence-composite modeling — provenance-aware synthesis of fragmented evidence into simulation-ready components.
